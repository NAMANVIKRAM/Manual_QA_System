## Definition of Product Validation and its Distinction from Product Verification

### Product Validation Definition

Product Validation is the process of determining that the product accomplishes its intended purpose in the intended environment. It ensures that the system functions as expected when placed in its intended environment, meeting the stakeholder expectations [Section 2.4, p.21; Section 5.4, p.109].

### Distinction from Product Verification

The key differences between Product Validation and Product Verification are:

* **Objective**:
	+ Verification: To show proof of compliance with requirements, ensuring that the product meets each "shall" statement.
	+ Validation: To show that the product accomplishes its intended purpose in the intended environment, meeting stakeholder expectations.
* **Focus**:
	+ Verification: Focuses on checking that the product is built correctly, adhering to the approved requirements set.
	+ Validation: Focuses on checking that the right product is built, ensuring it functions as expected in its intended environment.
* **Relation to Documentation**:
	+ Verification: Relates back to the approved requirements set, specifications, and configuration documentation.
	+ Validation: Relates back to the Concept of Operations (ConOps) document and stakeholder expectations.

In summary, verification ensures that the product is built correctly, while validation ensures that the right product is built [Section 2.4, p.21; Section 5.4, p.109].

**Key Decision Point (KDP) Overview**

* A Key Decision Point (KDP) is an event where the decision authority determines the readiness of a program/project to progress to the next phase of the life cycle [Section 2.7, p.23].
* KDPs are the points at which the decision authority decides whether to proceed with the project or terminate it.

**Decision-Making at KDPs**

* The decision to proceed or terminate a project is made by the Decision Authority, who is an individual authorized by the Agency to make important decisions for programs and projects under their authority [Section 6.8.2, p.180].

**KDP Process**

* At a KDP, the decision authority determines whether the program/project is ready to proceed to the next phase or not.
* If the project is not ready, it may be allowed to try again later after addressing deficiencies, or it may be terminated [Section 2.7, p.23].
* Updates to technical plans are also expected to occur at every KDP, as necessary, to reflect the current environment and resources and to control the project's performance, cost, and schedule [Section 6.1, p.123].

Q13 [TIER 3 - Acronyms] - What is the difference between a PDR and an SDR?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RETRIEVED SECTIONS: ['6.8', '4.4.1.2', '3.11.4.3']
PAGES: [170, 77, 47]
CHAPTERS: ['6', '4', '3']  CROSS-CHAPTER: YES
HOPS: 0 extra docs
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ANSWER:
The Preliminary Design Review (PDR) and the Program/System Definition Review (SDR) serve distinct purposes in the NASA Systems Engineering Handbook.

* **Preliminary Design Review (PDR):** 
  - Demonstrates that the preliminary design meets all system requirements with acceptable risk and within cost and schedule constraints.
  - Establishes the basis for proceeding with detailed design.
  - Occurs near the completion of the preliminary design phase (Phase B) as the last review in the Formulation Phase.
  - Entrance and success criteria are defined in Table G-6 of NPR 7123.1.
  - A successful PDR authorizes the project to proceed into the Implementation Phase and toward final design [Section 6.8, p.170].

* **Program/System Definition Review (SDR):** 
  - Evaluates whether the proposed architecture is responsive to the functional and performance requirements and that the requirements have been allocated to all functional elements of the mission/system.
  - Conducted during the concept development phase (Phase A) prior to KDP B and the start of preliminary design.
  - Entrance and success criteria for projects and single-project programs are defined in Table G-5 of NPR 7123.1.
  - A successful SDR supports the decision to further develop the system architecture/design and any technology needed to accomplish the mission [Section 6.8, p.170, Section 6.8, p.170].

In summary, the PDR focuses on the maturity of the preliminary design and its readiness to proceed to detailed design, while the SDR assesses the proposed system's architecture and its alignment with functional and performance requirements early in the project lifecycle.
